Healthcare RCM Application - Database Design Document

Version: 1.0

For: Development Team

1. Database Overview

Database Type: Relational Database (PostgreSQL recommended)

Why Relational: Healthcare data is highly structured with complex relationships

Total Tables: 35 core tables

Estimated Records (Medium Hospital):

  • Patients: 100,000+
  • Encounters: 500,000+ per year
  • Claims: 500,000+ per year
  • Payments: 1,000,000+ per year

    • 2. Core Database Tables

    3. Database Relationships

    Key Relationships:

  • patients → patient_insurance (1:Many) - One patient, multiple insurances
  • patients → encounters (1:Many) - One patient, many visits
  • encounters → charges (1:Many) - One encounter, many charges
  • encounters → claims (1:1 or 1:Many) - One encounter can generate multiple claims (primary, secondary)
  • claims → claim_lines (1:Many) - One claim, multiple line items
  • claims → payments (1:Many) - One claim can have multiple payments
  • claims → denials (1:1) - One claim, one denial record
  • denials → appeals (1:Many) - One denial, multiple appeal levels
  • providers → provider_credentials (1:Many) - One provider, credentialed with many payers
  • payers → payer_contracts (1:Many) - One payer, multiple contracts
  • payercontracts → payercontract_rates (1:Many) - One contract, many CPT rates

    • 4. Indexes & Performance

    Critical Indexes:

  • All foreign keys
  • Date fields used in queries (servicedate, paymentdate, etc.)
  • Status fields (claim.status, encounter.status)
  • Search fields (patient.last_name, patient.mrn)

    • Partitioning Strategy:

  • claims table: Partition by submission_date (monthly partitions)
  • payments table: Partition by payment_date (monthly partitions)
  • audit_logs table: Partition by timestamp (monthly partitions)

    • 5. Data Retention Policy

    Table Retention Period audit_logs 7 years (HIPAA) patients Indefinite encounters Indefinite claims 10 years payments 10 years denials 10 years appeals 10 years reports 3 years

    6. Security & Encryption

    Encrypted Fields:

  • patients.ssn_encrypted
  • providers.ssn_encrypted
  • Any PHI (Protected Health Information)

    • Encryption Method:

  • AES-256 encryption at rest
  • TLS 1.2+ for data in transit
  • Application-level encryption for SSN fields

    • 7. Backup Strategy

  • Full Backup: Daily at 2 AM
  • Incremental Backup: Every 4 hours
  • Transaction Log Backup: Every 15 minutes
  • Backup Retention: 30 days
  • Disaster Recovery: Geo-redundant backups in separate region
