4. MVP Workflow - Complete Step-by-Step Flow

Real-Time Example: Patient "Sarah Johnson" Visit

Let me explain the complete workflow using a real example:

**Step 1: Patient Registration** 📝

Scenario: Sarah Johnson calls to book her first appointment.

What Happens:

  • Front desk staff opens the application
  • Clicks "New Patient"
  • Enters Sarah's information:
  • Name: Sarah Johnson
  • DOB: March 15, 1985 (41 years old)
  • Phone: (555) 123-4567
  • Email: sarah.j@email.com
  • Address: 123 Main St, Austin, TX 78701
  • Insurance: Blue Cross Blue Shield
  • Policy Number: ABC123456789
  • Group Number: GRP5000
  • System generates Patient ID: PAT-2026-5678
  • Patient record saved ✅

    • Time Taken: 2-3 minutes

    **Step 2: Insurance Verification** 🔍

    What Happens:

  • Staff clicks "Verify Insurance" button
  • System sends request to Waystar API:

    • `

    Patient: Sarah Johnson

    DOB: 03/15/1985

    Insurance: Blue Cross Blue Shield

    Policy: ABC123456789

    Service Date: May 20, 2026

    `

  • Waystar sends EDI 270 to Blue Cross
  • Blue Cross responds with EDI 271 in 10 seconds
  • System displays results:

    • `

    ✅ Coverage Status: ACTIVE

    ✅ Copay: $25.00

    ✅ Deductible: $1,500 annual

  • Met: $800
  • Remaining: $700

    • ✅ Out-of-Pocket Max: $5,000

  • Met: $1,200
  • Remaining: $3,800

    • ✅ Network Status: IN-NETWORK

    ⚠️ Prior Auth: NOT REQUIRED for office visit

    `

  • Staff notes: "Collect $25 copay at check-in"

    • Time Taken: 30 seconds

    **Step 3: Appointment Scheduling** 📅

    What Happens:

  • Staff opens calendar
  • Selects date: May 20, 2026, 10:00 AM
  • Selects provider: Dr. Smith
  • Selects appointment type: "New Patient Office Visit"
  • Adds note: "Patient has cough and fever"
  • System creates appointment ✅
  • System sends SMS reminder via Twilio API (optional):

    • `

    "Hi Sarah, your appointment with Dr. Smith is on

    May 20 at 10:00 AM. Please bring your insurance card.

    Reply CONFIRM to confirm."

    `

    Time Taken: 1 minute

    **Step 4: Patient Check-In (Day of Visit)** ✅

    What Happens:

  • Sarah arrives at 9:50 AM
  • Front desk checks her in
  • Collects $25 copay (credit card via Stripe API)
  • System creates encounter record:

    • `

    Encounter ID: ENC-2026-9876

    Patient: Sarah Johnson (PAT-2026-5678)

    Provider: Dr. Smith

    Date: May 20, 2026

    Time: 10:00 AM

    Type: Office Visit

    Status: Checked In

    `

    Time Taken: 2 minutes

    **Step 5: Doctor Documentation** 👨‍⚕️

    What Happens:

  • Dr. Smith sees Sarah at 10:00 AM
  • Examines patient
  • Diagnosis: Acute bronchitis
  • Treatment: Prescribed antibiotics
  • Dr. Smith documents in EHR:

    • `

    Chief Complaint: Cough and fever for 5 days

    History: 41-year-old female presents with productive

    cough, yellow sputum, fever (101°F), chest discomfort.

    No shortness of breath.

    Examination:

  • Lungs: Rhonchi in both lower lobes
  • No wheezing
  • O2 saturation: 98%

    • Assessment: Acute bronchitis (J20.9)

    Plan:

  • Azithromycin 500mg daily x 5 days
  • Increase fluids
  • Follow up if symptoms worsen

    • `

  • Visit ends at 10:20 AM

    • Time Taken: 20 minutes

    **Step 6: Medical Coding** 🏥

    What Happens:

  • Medical coder opens encounter ENC-2026-9876
  • Reviews Dr. Smith's notes
  • AI-Assisted Coding (using OpenAI API):
  • System analyzes notes
  • Suggests codes:

    • `

    Suggested ICD-10:

    ✅ J20.9 - Acute bronchitis, unspecified (95% confidence)

    ✅ R05 - Cough (88% confidence)

    ✅ R50.9 - Fever, unspecified (85% confidence)

    Suggested CPT:

    ✅ 99203 - New patient office visit, moderate complexity

    `

  • Coder reviews and confirms:
  • ICD-10 Codes:
  • J20.9 (Primary diagnosis)
  • R05 (Secondary)
  • R50.9 (Secondary)
  • CPT Code:
  • 99203 (Office visit, 30-44 minutes)
  • Charge: $150.00
  • Codes saved to encounter ✅

    • Time Taken: 3 minutes (with AI assistance)

    Without AI: 10-15 minutes

    **Step 7: Claim Creation** 📄

    What Happens:

  • Biller opens "Claims" module
  • Selects encounter ENC-2026-9876
  • Clicks "Create Claim"
  • System auto-generates claim:

    • `

    Claim ID: CLM-2026-001234

    Format: CMS-1500 (Professional claim)

    Patient: Sarah Johnson

    DOB: 03/15/1985

    Insurance: Blue Cross Blue Shield

    Policy: ABC123456789

    Provider: Dr. Smith

    NPI: 1234567890

    Tax ID: 12-3456789

    Service Date: 05/20/2026

    Place of Service: 11 (Office)

    Diagnosis Codes:

  • J20.9 (Acute bronchitis)
  • R05 (Cough)
  • R50.9 (Fever)

    • Procedure:

    CPT: 99203

    Charge: $150.00

    Units: 1

    `

  • Claim Scrubbing (via Waystar API):
  • Validates all fields ✅
  • Checks code combinations ✅
  • Verifies medical necessity ✅
  • No errors found ✅
  • Claim status: "Ready to Submit"

    • Time Taken: 1 minute

    **Step 8: Claim Submission** 📤

    What Happens:

  • Biller selects claim CLM-2026-001234
  • Clicks "Submit Claim"
  • System converts to EDI 837 format
  • Sends to Waystar API
  • Waystar forwards to Blue Cross Blue Shield
  • Receives acknowledgment (EDI 999):

    • `

    Status: ACCEPTED

    Tracking Number: TRK-987654321

    Submission Date: 05/20/2026 11:30 AM

    `

  • Claim status updated: "Submitted" ✅

    • Time Taken: 30 seconds

    **Step 9: Claim Adjudication** ⏳

    What Happens (at Insurance Company):

  • Blue Cross receives claim on May 20
  • Processes claim (automated system)
  • Checks:
  • Patient eligibility ✅
  • Coverage for service ✅
  • Medical necessity ✅
  • Coding accuracy ✅
  • Approves claim on May 22
  • Sends payment on May 25

    • Time Taken: 5 days (industry average: 14-30 days)

    **Step 10: Payment Posting** 💰

    What Happens:

  • On May 25, system receives ERA (EDI 835) from Waystar API
  • System parses ERA:

    • `

    Claim: CLM-2026-001234

    Patient: Sarah Johnson

    Service Date: 05/20/2026

    Billed Amount: $150.00

    Allowed Amount: $120.00 (contracted rate)

    Insurance Paid: $95.00 (80% after copay)

    Patient Copay: $25.00 (already collected)

    Contractual Adjustment: -$30.00

    Adjustment Codes:

  • CO-45: Charge exceeds fee schedule (-$30.00)
  • PR-3: Copay amount ($25.00)

    • Total Paid: $95.00

    Patient Balance: $0.00 (copay already collected)

    `

  • System auto-matches payment to claim
  • Biller reviews and posts payment
  • Claim status: "PAID" ✅
  • Updates accounts receivable

    • Time Taken: 1 minute

  const [patient, setPatient] = useState({
    firstName: '',
    lastName: '',
    dob: '',
    insurance: ''
  });
  const handleSubmit = async () => {
    const response = await fetch('/api/v1/patients', {
      method: 'POST',
      body: JSON.stringify(patient)
    });
    // Handle response
  };
  return (
    <form onSubmit={handleSubmit}>
      <input 
        placeholder="First Name"
        value={patient.firstName}
        onChange={(e) => setPatient({...patient, firstName: e.target.value})}
      />
      {/* More fields */}
      <button type="submit">Save Patient</button>
    </form>
  );
}

    • Database

    **PostgreSQL** 🐘

    Why PostgreSQL?

  • Reliable & Proven
  • 30+ years of development
  • Used by Apple, Instagram, Spotify
  • ACID compliant (data integrity guaranteed)
  • Perfect for Healthcare Data
  • Handles complex relationships (patients, claims, payments)
  • JSON support for flexible data
  • Full-text search for patient records
  • Scalable
  • Handles millions of records
  • Supports partitioning for large tables
  • Replication for high availability
  • Advanced Features
  • Foreign keys and constraints
  • Triggers for business rules
  • Views for complex queries
  • Excellent performance

    • Example Database Schema:

    
-- Patients table
CREATE TABLE patients (
    patient_id VARCHAR(50) PRIMARY KEY,
    first_name VARCHAR(100) NOT NULL,
    last_name VARCHAR(100) NOT NULL,
    dob DATE NOT NULL,
    ssn VARCHAR(11) ENCRYPTED,
    created_at TIMESTAMP DEFAULT NOW()
);
-- Claims table
CREATE TABLE claims (
    claim_id VARCHAR(50) PRIMARY KEY,
    patient_id VARCHAR(50) REFERENCES patients(patient_id),
    service_date DATE NOT NULL,
    billed_amount DECIMAL(10,2),
    paid_amount DECIMAL(10,2),
    status VARCHAR(20),
    created_at TIMESTAMP DEFAULT NOW()
);
-- Index for fast lookups
CREATE INDEX idx_claims_patient ON claims(patient_id);
CREATE INDEX idx_claims_status ON claims(status);
